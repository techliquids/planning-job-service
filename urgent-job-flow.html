⚡ Urgent Job Notification System

Real-time job distribution with intelligent escalation logic, batch notifications, and smart worker matching based on proximity, ratings, and availability.

📋 Complete Flow Sequence

1

Employer Posts Urgent Job

Employer creates and publishes an urgent job with title, description, location, budget, and time requirements.

Data Captured: Job title, category, location (GPS), budget, urgency level, time frame, attachments
2

System Detects Urgent Job Flag

System identifies the job as "urgent" and activates the escalation workflow instead of normal job posting.

Triggered Actions: Load worker database, calculate distances, sort by priority factors (rating, availability, distance)
3

Identify First Group of Workers

System identifies the first group (typically 3-5 workers) based on proximity, highest ratings, and availability status.

Selection Criteria: Distance (within X km) → Rating (⭐⭐⭐⭐ and above) → Online Status → Experience in category
4

Send Instant Notifications

Send push notifications, SMS, and in-app alerts to the first group simultaneously with job details.

Notification Channels: Push notification (instant), SMS alert (backup), In-app message, Email (asynchronous)
5

Wait for Timeout Period

System waits for a predefined time (usually 5 minutes) for workers to respond with proposals.

Monitoring: Track response time, count proposals received, monitor worker interactions
6

Decision Point: Proposals Received?

Check if any workers have sent proposals within the timeout period.

YES: Proceed to Step 8 | NO: Proceed to Step 7
7

Escalate to Next Group

If no proposals in timeout period, automatically escalate to the next group of workers with slightly lower priority criteria.

Next Group Selection: Expand distance radius → Include slightly lower-rated workers → Include semi-available workers → Repeat process
8

Employer Reviews Proposals

Employer receives and reviews all proposals from interested workers, comparing prices, experience, and ratings.

Available Actions: Accept one proposal → Reject other proposals → Chat with workers → Request modifications
9

Employer Selects Worker

Employer accepts one proposal and job is assigned to that worker. Other proposals are automatically rejected.

Notifications Sent: Acceptance notification to selected worker → Rejection notifications to other workers
10

Worker Accepts Job & Travels

Selected worker accepts the job, gets location details, and travels to the job site with real-time tracking.

Tracking: GPS location sharing → Real-time navigation → Employer can track arrival → ETA updates
11

Job Execution & Payment

Worker performs the service, completes the job, and payment is processed through the wallet system.

Process: Worker check-in → Perform job → Worker check-out → Employer confirmation → Payment to wallet → Commission deduction

🎯 Escalation Group Strategy

Progressive Escalation Groups

Group 1 (0-5 min)

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4-5 star workers
📍 Closest location
🟢 Online status
✅ High experience
5 min wait

Group 2 (5-10 min)

⭐⭐⭐⭐ 3-4 star workers
📍 Within 10 km
🟡 Semi-available
✅ Good experience
Next escalate

Group 3 (10-15 min)

⭐⭐⭐ 3+ star workers
📍 Within 20 km
🟡 Somewhat available
✅ Moderate experience
Next escalate

Group 4+ (15+ min)

⭐⭐ 2+ star workers
📍 Extended radius
🔴 Offline but reachable
⚠️ Any experience level
Final escalate
💡 Priority Algorithm:

Distance (40%) + Rating (35%) + Availability (15%) + Experience (10%) = Priority Score

🔀 Normal Job vs Urgent Job

Aspect Normal Job Urgent Job
Visibility Listed in job feed, searchable Instant notifications to selected workers
Target Audience All interested workers Pre-filtered top workers (by rating, distance, availability)
Response Time Open-ended (24-48 hours typical) Urgent (5-15 minutes with escalation)
Notification Method In-app notification only Push + SMS + In-app + Email (multi-channel)
Matching Algorithm Keyword-based search AI-based smart matching (distance, rating, availability)
Escalation No automatic escalation Auto-escalation to next group after timeout
Cost Implication Standard commission rate May have premium rate or dynamic pricing
Bidding Process Multiple bids from interested workers Targeted bids from shortlisted workers
Acceptance Rate Lower (more competition) Higher (pre-selected, nearby, available workers)
Success Rate Moderate (depends on market) Very High (multiple escalation groups)
